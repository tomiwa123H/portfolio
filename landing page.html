The House, London

Not fashion.
A position.

AURELIA was founded in 2020 on a single refusal: to participate in the cycle of excess. No weekly drops. No trend-chasing. No discounting what we made last month.

Every collection begins not with a mood board, but with a question — what does a woman actually need to own forever? The answer is always fewer things, made better.

Our story
Chapter I

The trouser
that holds
everything.

We started with trousers because we believed most women already owned too many — and that every single one was slightly wrong. Too stiff. Too formal. Too casual. Cut for a body that wasn't theirs.

The Aurelia Tailored Trousers — £120 took eleven months to get right. Italian wool-blend, structured waistband, wide-leg silhouette. The kind of trouser you put on and feel, immediately, like yourself.

We've made them in three colourways this season: Stone, Onyx, Forest. They pair with everything and answer to nothing. This is the piece that started the brand.

Chapter II

Architecture
for the body.

A jacket has to do two contradictory things simultaneously: hold its shape and allow complete freedom. Most don't manage either. The shoulder collapses by Tuesday. The lining pulls by Thursday. You stop reaching for it.

The Structured Wool Jacket — £140 uses a half-canvas construction borrowed from bespoke tailoring. Not because it's more expensive to make — it is — but because it's the only way the shoulder works correctly in five years, not just five wears.

It buttons. It doesn't need to. We made it button anyway, because a jacket should have resolve.

Chapter III

The knit
that stays.

There is a particular failure mode for knitwear: it looks perfect in September and by December it's pilling, stretching, losing the plot. You didn't mistreat it. You just wore it. That should be enough.

The Minimal Knit Jumper — £80 uses a reinforced construction at every stress point. Shoulders, cuffs, hem. GOTS-certified merino, 14-gauge, brushed lightly on the inside. It's the kind of knit that improves with time — that softens into itself.

Four colourways. All muted. None of them trend.

What we stand for Est. London, 2020
I

Certified materials, always.

GOTS organic cotton. Responsible wool. TENCEL™ lyocell. We work only with certified mills. No exceptions for cost, no exceptions for convenience.

Materials
II

Designed for ten years.

Every brief begins: will a woman still reach for this in a decade? If the answer isn't a clear yes, we start again. We don't design for seasons. We design for wardrobes.

Longevity
III

Small batches, skilled hands.

Production in Porto, finishing in London. Fewer than 400 pieces per season. We don't scale what can't scale well. Each piece is made with the same attention as the sample.

Production
IV

Lifetime repair, no questions.

We repair every piece we've ever made — free for manufacturing faults, at cost for wear. Because ownership shouldn't end when a seam gives way.
