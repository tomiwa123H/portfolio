The House of AURELIA

A refusal
to participate
in excess.

Founded in an East London studio in 2020. Seven pieces. One question: will she still reach for this in five years?

2020

The wardrobe full of clothes and nothing to wear.

Creative Director Mara Ellison stood in front of a full wardrobe and felt nothing. Not emptiness — she owned plenty. But none of it was right. Everything was made to be replaced. Designed for seasons, not for lives. Made in factories that moved as fast as the demand required.

AURELIA was her correction. Not a brand in the conventional sense — a commitment. To make fewer things, better. To design with the assumption that the woman buying them will still own them in ten years and will still be glad she did.

The first collection was seven pieces. They sold out in four days. The model has never changed.

Mara Ellison

Creative Director, London

Porto, Portugal

How it's made

Made slowly.
On purpose.

Each AURELIA collection begins six months before it reaches you. Fabric sourcing alone takes eight weeks — we work only with mills holding current GOTS certification, primarily in Italy, Japan, and Portugal.

Production is limited by design. Every sample is worn by the team for a minimum of four weeks. If it doesn't become a first reach, it doesn't ship. No exceptions — not for cost, not for time, not for the number of pieces we could sell if we just moved faster.

Fewer than 400 pieces per season. Not as a premium signal. Because it's the only way to make each one well.

Materials

Certified, or not at all.

GOTS organic cotton. Responsible wool. TENCEL™ lyocell. We work only with certified mills. No exceptions for cost, no exceptions for convenience. If a material doesn't meet certification, it doesn't enter a design brief.

We publish our full supply chain on request. Every mill, every dye house, every shipping route. Not because we're required to — because it's the only honest way to operate.
